# Case Study: Il posto delle fragole
## Building a Living Brand Identity for a Community Space

**Domain**: Social innovation · Community space
**Location**: Via Ponchielli 81, Pesaro (Quartiere San Martino)
**Challenge**: Build a shared brand identity from the values of four partner organizations for a new community space for young people
**Specula Method phases applied**: Phase 1.5, Phase 2, Phase 3 (partial), Phase 4
**Timeline**: October 2025 – February 2026

---

## The Challenge

Four organizations — ARCI, APS LiberaMusica, Cooperativa Labirinto, Fondazione Wanda di Ferdinando — were building a new community space in Pesaro's San Martino neighbourhood. The space had a location, a provisional working group, and a strong shared commitment to young people.

What it didn't have: a coherent identity.

Each partner brought different histories, mandates, and visions. The risk was the usual one: a space defined by the sum of its services (kitchen, music studio, tech lab, event space) rather than by its distinctive character. A brand that could list activities, but couldn't say what kind of presence it wanted to be.

The deeper question wasn't "what will this place do?" It was: what kind of space does this place refuse not to be?

---

## The Process

### Phase 0 — Co-design as raw material

Before any brand work, Sineglossa facilitated three participatory workshops with the four partner organizations (October–November 2025). This wasn't background research — it became the primary source material for the entire identity.

**Workshop 1 — Values**: Partners mapped shared strengths across nine clusters: Contemporaneo, Arti, Futuri, Prossimità, Accoglienza e Inclusione, Sostenibilità, Cura, Competenze, Strutture. The diversity of inputs was instructive: care and innovation appeared equally, sometimes in tension.

**Workshop 2 — Collage**: Each person chose an image element that symbolised something essential. The collective collage — named *Zuppa* by the group — produced metaphors that proved more durable than any explicit statement: the butterfly (fragility as feature, not flaw), the mushroom (protection that generates ecosystems), the house with window (intimacy without performance).

**Workshop 3 — Strategy**: A speculative exercise — "Zuppa in copertina" — asked the group to imagine a neighbourhood magazine cover about the space. This produced the first draft of Vision, Mission, and audience segmentation.

---

### Phase 1.5 — Competitive Futures Mapping

The neighbourhood context was mapped — not to benchmark competitors, but to find the *futures white spaces*: what the area already had (sportello sociale, churches, skate park, schools), what was recently lost (a well-attended community centre), and what the emerging territory wanted (intergenerational meeting, safe creative risk, technology access).

The conclusion: no one in the neighbourhood was credibly presiding over *psychological safety as the condition for creative risk*. This became the strategic white space.

---

### Phase 2 — Brand Archeology

The co-design output was rich but unstructured. The Brand Archeology phase translated it from descriptive to operational.

**The key tension**: the most significant polarity in the material was between *cura* (care, protection, warmth) and *innovazione* (technology, creative risk, experimentation). A simpler process would have resolved this by choosing one side. Instead, the Ethical Gate asked: is this a contradiction to resolve, or a productive polarity to inhabit?

The answer: redefine care not as protection, but as *infrastructure that enables power*. Psychological safety is the condition that makes creative risk possible — not its opposite.

This moved the brand from welfare space to *enabling space*: access to powerful tools in a non-judgmental environment.

**Radical values — from descriptive to operational:**

| Value | Descriptive form (co-design) | Operational form (governance) |
|---|---|---|
| Abitabilità | "Uno spazio in cui si sta bene" | If it doesn't improve the quality of being together, it's not a valid choice |
| Non giudizio | "Uno spazio sicuro e accessibile" | People come before labels; clear limits without stigma |
| Abilitazione | "Accesso a strumenti e competenze" | Care is infrastructure, not assistance |
| Immaginazione in pratica | "Sperimentazione e rischio creativo" | Imagination is trained here — not escaped into |
| Comunità viva | "Relazioni e appartenenza" | We don't consume experiences; we cultivate returns |

### Phase 3 — Ethical Gate and Registry of Refusals

The brand defines not just what the space is, but what it refuses to become.

**Registry of Refusals:**
- Spaces where success is measured only by attendance numbers
- Programming that positions adults as more important than young people's ownership
- Visual or communication languages "too reassuring for adults" at the cost of being unrecognisable to the Gen Z primary audience
- Branding that promises performance rather than presence

**Structural tension logged (not resolved)**: the name *Il posto delle fragole* carries Bergman associations that resonate with adult stakeholders and partners, but may not land immediately with the 15–23 primary audience. This was recorded as a known tension requiring ongoing attention in communication strategy — not resolved by renaming.

---

### Phase 4 — Narrative Synthesis

The Brand Manual translates the archeology work into a coherent system across three layers.

**1. Foundations** — Vision, Mission, Purpose, Brand Values, Tone of Voice.
The brand is defined as an *organismo* (living organism), not a container. The distinction matters: an organism has a character that persists even when activities change.

**2. Identity system** — Logo and visual language.
The logo mark uses three forms (elongated ellipse, oval, circle) representing three ways of inhabiting the space: *Esploratori* (discovering — energy in movement), *Attivatori* (producing — energy structures), *Garanti* (enabling — energy stabilises). These are not fixed roles — they are traversable states.

The system evolves through four named configurations:
- **Stato denso** — when the space is full of relationships
- **Stato gestuale** — when something spontaneous happens
- **Stato modulare** — when the community builds together
- **Stato essenziale** — when only what matters remains

Identity that moves, but doesn't disperse.

**3. Color as governance** — Sistema cromatico per interni.
The color system makes values operational at the spatial level. Sand (60%) builds the habitable base. Green and Black (35%) orient without imposing. Red (5%) activates without saturating. Each color carries an explicit operational rule. Sand is not neutral background — it is the actual space where everything happens.

The principle: color doesn't serve to be noticed. It serves to make the space work.

---

## What the Specula Method enabled

Without the method, the four organizations would likely have produced a reasonable shared brand — a logo, a color palette, a mission statement. The Specula Method produced something different: a **governance system for identity**.

The brand manual is not just instructions for designers. It is a decision framework. When a new activity is proposed, the values matrix determines whether it fits. When communication choices arise, the tone of voice axes define what is allowed. When the space evolves, the living system gives it permission to change states without losing itself.

The co-design process provided the raw values. Brand Archeology made them operational. The Ethical Gate resolved the key tension. Narrative Synthesis built a system capable of persisting.
